About Haruki Murakami

Haruki Murakami is a Japanese writer known for his use of magical realism. He was born in Kyoto, Japan, in 1949. He originally opened a jazz cafe with his wife after graduating from college, but reportedly began his writing career in 1978, after he had an epiphany while attending a baseball game that he could write a novel. He began writing that night.

Murakami's works have been widely sold both within Japan and internationally, with his writing having been translated into 50+ languages. Some of his most well known works include Hard-Boiled Wonderland and the End of the World, Norwegian Wood, Kafka on the Shore, 1Q84, and The Wind-Up Bird Chronicle. His fictional works are known for carrying overarching themes of "missing something and seeking it and finding it" (Murakami, in an interview with The New Yorker).
